Print the Quiet

Nine essays on tone, dynamics, and the parts of a record that never make it onto the lead sheet.

There is a breath at 3:20 in Jeff Buckley's Hallelujah — a body, audible inside the tape, that no lead sheet contains. This is a nine-part listen for the things that don't fit on the chart: the Telecaster and the Quadraverb preset, the loudness war in DR numbers, the religion of the 1176, the rooms at Bearsville and Muscle Shoals, the difference between a comp of takes and a stitch of syllables. Free to read, and a companion to the book THE SIGNAL CHAIN.
